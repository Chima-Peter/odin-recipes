Jollof Rice

Description

Jollof rice is a rice dish from West Africa. Many would argue it first originated from Senegal, but others swear it's from Nigeria. No matter the origin, Jolof is one dish that binds us all across Africa, and it's swiftly gaining international recognition and popularity.

Nigerian Jollof is a stewed base rice dish made with three main ingredients: rice, tomato/pepper- stew base, and African-style seasonings like curry powder, dried thyme, and chicken stock.

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Tomatoes/Paste
  • Bell Peppers
  • Scotch Bonnet Peppers
  • Onions
  • Vegetables
  • Spices

Steps

  1. Add onions, tomatoes, bell peppers, and ginger in a blender. Reserve some onions and tomatoes for sautéing garnish
  2. Blend the tomatoes, peppers, onions, and ginger until smooth or roughly blended (or to your preference).
  3. Heat oil, sauté onions, and bay leaf until fragrant. Add tomato paste, pour in the blended mix, and bring to a boil. Season with cube, salt, curry, thyme, and liquid smoke. Stir well to combine.
  4. Add the rice and stock. Stir ONCE in a steady stream. Cover the pot with aluminum foil and then with the lid. Lower heat to medium-low and simmer.
  5. Add basil, thinly sliced onion, and tomatoes. Stir and cover with the lid to cook some more.
  6. Scoop the rice and check for doneness. Add the butter and fluff with a fork. Remove from heat and serve.
Ta-da! Pipping hot, delicious, smoky jollof rice ready to be devoured. I love to serve it alongside fried plantain or chicken. It's a match made in heaven.
